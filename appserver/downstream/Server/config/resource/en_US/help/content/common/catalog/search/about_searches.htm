Tips   About searching for catalog items

Your catalog contains specially priced items that save the company money. It may also contain links to supplier Internet Catalogs that contain discounted items. When you purchase catalog items, you help the company control costs.

If you change:

  • Search options, your changes remain in effect until you change or reset them.
  • View By or Show Results settings, the page refreshes using the new settings.

If you cannot find what you need in the catalog, you can request a non-catalog item. See Add non-catalog items for instructions.

See Explore catalogs for additional search tips and instructions.

When creating a request for goods or services, search your company catalog to find just the right item. Use a simple text search and add options as necessary to refine the search. You can save your search to use in the future and modify a saved search.

For efficient text searches:

  • Think of different words to use. What one or two key word(s) would you use to describe the item?
  • Use specific words instead of general ones.
  • For automatic wildcard searching, enter characters for text searches or alphanumeric characters for part number searches. By default, you must enter a minimum of three consecutive characters.
  • Use a stem search to locate similar items grouped by part number.

Any punctuation in keyword strings is treated as white space. This includes punctuation marks such as dash, quotes, percent signs and parentheses, and special characters such as the asterisk.

When your search returns results, the number of items found displays above the results list. If no results display, you can refine your search, browse through the catalog, or request a non-catalog item.
